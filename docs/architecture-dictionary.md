# Aletheia Architecture Dictionary

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Approved
**Release:** 0.1.0
**Sprint:** 0 – Foundation

---

# 1. Purpose

The Aletheia Architecture Dictionary establishes a shared vocabulary for the platform.

It provides the official meaning of architectural, engineering, and AI concepts used throughout the project.

All documentation, architecture, code, discussions, and future design decisions should use the definitions contained in this document.

This dictionary is a living reference and will evolve together with Aletheia.

---

# 2. How to Use This Dictionary

Each entry contains:

* **Category** – The domain to which the term belongs.
* **Definition** – The official meaning within Aletheia.
* **Architect's Note** – Why the concept matters.
* **Example** – A practical example.
* **Related Terms** – Connected concepts.
* **Stability** – Likelihood that the definition will change.

---

# 3. Stability Levels

| Rating | Meaning                                                     |
| ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| ★★★★★  | Fundamental architectural concept. Very unlikely to change. |

# 4. Architecture Terms

## Architecture

**Category**

Architecture

**Definition**

The fundamental structure of Aletheia describing its capabilities, responsibilities, relationships, and guiding principles independently of implementation technologies.

**Architect's Note**

Architecture should remain stable while implementation evolves.

**Related Terms**

Capability, Layer, Platform

**Stability**

★★★★★

---

## Capability

**Category**

Architecture

**Definition**

A reusable unit of business value representing what the platform can accomplish independently of implementation.

**Architect's Note**

Capabilities describe **what** the platform does, never **how** it does it.

**Example**

Knowledge Retrieval

Content Generation

Decision Support

**Related Terms**

Layer, Workflow, Agent

**Stability**

★★★★★

---

## Layer

**Category**

Architecture

**Definition**

A logical grouping of related capabilities sharing a common responsibility.

**Architect's Note**

Layers organize complexity while preserving separation of concerns.

**Related Terms**

Capability, Platform

**Stability**

★★★★★

---

## Platform

**Category**

Architecture

**Definition**

A reusable foundation supporting multiple capabilities and future extensions.

**Architect's Note**

Aletheia is designed as a platform rather than a single application.

**Stability**

★★★★★

---

## Workflow

**Category**

Architecture

**Definition**

A sequence of capabilities executed to achieve a specific objective.

**Related Terms**

Capability, Decision, Agent

**Stability**

★★★★★

# 5. Artificial Intelligence Terms

## Agent

**Category**

Artificial Intelligence

**Definition**

An autonomous software component responsible for executing one or more capabilities.

**Architect's Note**

An Agent is an implementation construct, not an architectural capability.

**Related Terms**

Capability, Workflow

**Stability**

★★★★☆

---

## Large Language Model (LLM)

**Category**

Artificial Intelligence

**Definition**

A language model capable of understanding and generating natural language.

**Architect's Note**

LLMs are replaceable technologies.

Architecture must never depend on a specific model.

**Related Terms**

Prompt, Context

**Stability**

★★☆☆☆

---

## Prompt

**Category**

Artificial Intelligence

**Definition**

Instructions provided to an AI model to guide its behavior.

**Related Terms**

LLM, Context

**Stability**

★★☆☆☆

---

## Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

**Category**

Artificial Intelligence

**Definition**

A technique that combines external knowledge retrieval with language model generation to improve factual accuracy.

**Architect's Note**

RAG grounds AI responses in trusted knowledge.

**Related Terms**

Knowledge, Retrieval, LLM

**Stability**

★★★☆☆

---

## Hallucination

**Category**

Artificial Intelligence

**Definition**

A fabricated or incorrect response generated by an AI model while appearing factual.

**Architect's Note**

Reducing hallucinations is one of the primary objectives of Aletheia.

**Related Terms**

Knowledge, Validation, RAG

**Stability**

★★★☆☆

# 7. Aletheia Terms

## Knowledge Layer

The architectural layer responsible for acquiring, organizing, validating, and maintaining trusted knowledge.

---

## Intelligence Layer

Transforms knowledge into reasoning through analysis, planning, summarization, and synthesis.

---

## Decision Layer

Coordinates workflows, business rules, approvals, and execution.

---

## Delivery Layer

Produces reports, articles, presentations, documentation, and other approved outputs.

---

## Learning Layer

Collects feedback, measures quality, and enables continuous improvement.

---

## Governance Layer

Provides security, compliance, auditability, traceability, and policy enforcement across the platform.
